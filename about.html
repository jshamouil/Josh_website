About Me

Hi, my name is Josh, and I am a data scientist with experience in investing and insurance. I have completed interesting projects through academic and professional career that have allowed me to explore topics such as prediction and forecasting using regression, time series, statistical modeling to use these tools to form a story about data.

I started began academic career at Rutgers with a bachelor’s degree in mathematics. While I was an undergraduate student, I completed interesting courses such as mathematical finance and stochastic processes and projects including stock option volatility analysis and a prediction on wages. After I completed my undergraduate degree in 2017, I went on to complete a master’s degree at Rutgers in Financial Statistics and Risk Management. During my time as a master’s student, I was involved in the Quantitative Finance Club where I held an executive position and gave multiple lectures on subjects including regression and time series. I also completed a few interesting projects, some of which can be found in the Projects tab.

After graduating in 2018 I began working at a financial technology firm called Markov Processes International as a Quantitative Analyst. I specialized in their proprietary software that allow users to analyze and report on different indices, mutual funds, and hedge funds. While here I got hands on experience in asset allocation, financial simulation, reporting, and statistical analysis by working with our clients from Fortune 500 companies. In September of 2019 I transitioned to CNO Financial, an insurance company, as a Marketing Statistician where I currently work. Here I develop classification machine learning models to predict which prospects and customers we should market to. I also carefully construct each dataset for our models by understanding constraints and mechanics of our marketing campaigns and by the behavior of the customers within our market by doing robust analysis.
